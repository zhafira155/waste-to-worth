Jerrycan Waste


Jerrycan waste is leftover packaging from product processing that is no longer used but can still be utilizied again. Jerrycan waste generally comes from plastic materials that have experien -ced physical or chemical changes due to use and are no longer suitable for their original function. This type of waste can be classified as B3 waste (hazardous and toxic materials) if it contains chemical residues that are harmful to humans and the environment.


Jerrycan waste can be used in various sectors, such as plantatiom. agriculture, and household needs. In agriculture, used jerrycan can be reused as containers for water or fertilizers. In the household sector, it can be repurposed as plant pots or storage containers. However, the use of jerrycan waste must be carried out carefully. if the jerrycan previously contained hazardous materials, It must first be cleaned thoroughly to remove harmful residues failure to property clean them can lead to enviromental and health risks.


One of the efforts to manage jerrycan waste is through such as buckets, plastic ropes, or handicraft products that have economic value. This process not only reduces waste but also creates new job opportunities. Nevertheless, jerrycan waste still causes problems if not managed properly. Many people dispose of jerrycan waste carelessly. which can pollute the environment. Plastic jerrycan that decomposes slowy can clog water ways, kill marine life, and reduce soil fertility.


Therefore, awareness and participation from the public. industry. and goverment are needed to manage jerrycan waste wirely. Proper jerrycan waste management can provide many benefits. nor only for the environment but also economically and Socially.The price is quite high, but not too expensive depending on the material quality. However, it still depends on how the jerrycan is used, whether it is damaged or sill in good condition.
